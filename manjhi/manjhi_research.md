# Dashrath Manjhi — Research Notes
*The "Mountain Man" of India*

---

## Who Was He?

Dashrath Manjhi was a real person who lived in India. He is famous for doing something that most people said was impossible — and he did it completely by himself. He is sometimes called the **"Mountain Man of India"** or **"Mountain Man of Bihar."**

---

## Early Life

- Manjhi was born on **January 14, 1934**, in a small, rocky village called **Gehlaur** in the state of Bihar, India.
- His family was very poor. They belonged to a group called the **Musahar community**, which was one of the most disadvantaged groups in India at the time.
- As a young boy, Manjhi did not have the chance to go to school. He never learned to read or write.
- Life in Gehlaur was very hard. The village was cut off from the nearest town by a steep, rocky mountain. To reach the town — which had hospitals, schools, and markets — villagers had to walk around the mountain, a journey of over **55 kilometers** (about 34 miles).
- As a child, Manjhi eventually **ran away from home** to find work. He traveled to a faraway city called Dhanbad, where he worked in **coal mines** to survive.
- After a few years, he came back to his village, married a woman named **Falguni Devi**, and worked as a daily laborer on farms.

---

## The Event That Changed His Life

In **1959**, something very sad happened that changed Manjhi forever.

His wife Falguni was bringing him food while he was working on the far side of the mountain. She slipped on the dangerous rocky path and was badly hurt. Because the nearest hospital was so far away — over 55 kilometers — she could not get medical help in time, and she died.

Manjhi was heartbroken. He was also angry — not at a person, but at the **mountain itself**. He made a promise that no other family in his village would ever suffer the same way.

So in **1960**, he bought a hammer, a chisel, and a crowbar (he sold his family's goats to pay for them) — and he started chipping away at the mountain. All by himself.

---

## His Life's Work

- For **22 years** — from 1960 to 1982 — Manjhi chipped away at the rocky hill every single day.
- People in the village thought he was crazy. They laughed at him and called him a lunatic.
- But he never stopped.
- When he was done, he had carved a road **110 meters long, 9.1 meters wide, and 7.6 meters deep** through solid rock — using only hand tools.
- His road cut the distance to the nearest town from **55 kilometers down to just 15 kilometers**.
- Now the people in his village could reach a hospital, a school, and a market much more easily.

---

## His Impact and Legacy

- After he finished the road, Manjhi spent the rest of his life asking the government to build better roads, schools, and hospitals for poor villages like his.
- In **2006**, the Chief Minister of Bihar was so moved by Manjhi's story that he stood up and offered Manjhi his own chair as a sign of deep respect — something almost unheard of.
- Manjhi passed away on **August 17, 2007**, from cancer. He was 73 years old.
- The Government of Bihar gave him a **state funeral** — a special honor usually reserved for very important leaders.
- The road he carved was officially paved and named the **Dashrath Manjhi Path** in his honor.
- A hospital and a memorial were built in his village.
- In **2016**, India Post printed a postage stamp with his face on it.
- A Bollywood movie called *Manjhi: The Mountain Man* (2015) told his story to the world.

## Things People Say He Was Like (Character)

Think about what kind of person it takes to do what Manjhi did. Some words people use to describe him:

- **Determined** — he never gave up, even when people laughed at him
- **Selfless** — he started for his wife, but kept going for his whole community
- **Hardworking** — he chipped at rock for 22 years without stopping
- **Brave** — he did something no one believed was possible
- **Humble** — he never wanted money or fame, only help for his village

## Famous Quotes

Here are some things Dashrath Manjhi is remembered for saying:

> *"When I started hammering the hill, people called me a lunatic, but that steeled my resolve."*

> *"I started this work out of love for my wife, but continued it for my people. If I did not, no one would."*

> *"Never depend upon God. Who knows, maybe God is depending upon you."*

> *"The wound is in the heart — it won't heal until the mountain is broken."*
