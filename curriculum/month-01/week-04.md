# Month 1, Week 4 — CVC Blending: -ug, -un, -ut, -ed, -eg Families / Numerals 16–20 + Ordinals

## Goals
- Reading: Blend CVC words with short-u and short-e vowels; read 20+ total CVC words fluently across all five vowel families.
- Math: Recognize, read, and write numerals 16–20; use ordinal language (1st, 2nd, 3rd, 4th, 5th) in context.

## Day 1 — The -ug and -un Families

**Reading (8 min):** Write on cards: *bug, dug, hug, jug, mug, rug, tug* and *bun, fun, gun, nun, pun, run, sun*. Say, "New vowel — short-u says /u/ like *umbrella*! Listen: /b/... /u/... /g/... bug!" Blend *hug, mug, rug* together. Then: "Same /u/ sound, new ending — /un/! /r/... /un/... run!" Blend *fun, sun, bun*. Say, "Do you hear the same /u/ in bug and fun? Same vowel, different family!"
**Math (5 min):** Introduce 16 and 17. Show 16 objects (use two groups: a ten-stick and 6 loose): "Ten and six is sixteen — 16!" Write and trace 16. Then 17: "Ten and seven is seventeen — 17!" Trace 17. Ask, "What's hiding inside six-TEEN?" (Six!) Use the egg carton ten-frame if available.
**App:** Phonics Blender — blend *bug, run, hug, sun* with letter-by-letter taps.
**Reward:** A "Short-u Superstar" sticker for each -ug or -un word read correctly (up to seven).

## Day 2 — The -ut Family + Ordinals Introduction

**Reading (8 min):** Write on cards: *but, cut, gut, hut, nut, rut*. Say, "Another /u/ family — /ut/! /c/... /ut/... cut!" Blend *but, hut, nut, rut* together. Then do a quick three-family mix (-ug, -un, -ut): shuffle all cards and flip 12 random ones. Child blends each one. Say, "All three families have the same /u/ in the middle — you're an expert at short-u now!"
**Math (5 min):** Introduce ordinal numbers using a fun "race" setup. Line up five toys and say, "The bear finished FIRST — 1st! The bunny finished SECOND — 2nd! The frog is THIRD — 3rd!" Point to each toy and say its ordinal position. Ask, "Which one is 4th? Which one is 5th?" Use the words *first, second, third* in everyday context: "You got dressed — that was your FIRST job this morning!"
**App:** Lesson Player — loads an ordinal-position game where characters line up and child taps the correct position.
**Reward:** A "Race Winner" ribbon sticker — they write "1st" on it themselves.

## Day 3 — The -ed and -eg Families

**Reading (8 min):** Write on cards: *bed, fed, led, red, wed* and *beg, leg, peg, reg*. Say, "Last new vowel of the month — short-e says /e/ like *egg*! Listen: /r/... /e/... /d/... red!" Blend *bed, fed, led*. Then: "New family — /eg/! /l/... /eg/... leg!" Blend *beg, peg*. Do a "short-e freeze dance" — when you say a word with /e/, they freeze. Words to call: *red, hot, beg, sun, bed, cat, leg*. Say, "Freeze when you hear the /e/!"
**Math (5 min):** Introduce 18 and 19. Show 18 objects: "Ten and eight is eighteen — 18!" Write and trace. Then 19: "Ten and nine is nineteen — 19!" Trace. Play "Race to 20" — take turns rolling a die (1–6) and counting up from 10, writing each number as you land on it. First to write 19 wins. Say, "You're almost at 20 — the biggest teen number!"
**App:** Phonics Blender — blend *red, bed, leg, beg* and listen for the short-e sound.
**Reward:** A "Five-Vowel" badge — a special badge for knowing all five short vowels (a, e, i, o, u).

## Day 4 — All Five Vowel Families Grand Mix

**Reading (8 min):** Shuffle a set of 25 cards pulled from all five vowel families across the month (-at/-an/-ap, -ig/-in/-it, -ot/-op, -ug/-un/-ut, -ed/-eg). Flip one at a time. Child reads each word. Keep a tally of "first-try reads" — the goal is 20 out of 25. If they miss one, say, "Let's check the vowel — what's in the middle?" then work through it. End by counting the tally: "You read [X] words — that's incredible!"
**Math (5 min):** Introduce 20. Show 20 objects (two groups of ten): "Two full tens is twenty — 20!" Write and trace 20. Then do an ordinal challenge with the number line: point to numbers 11–20 written on a strip of paper and ask, "Which number is 3rd? Which is 6th?" They count from 11 and say the ordinal position. Say, "Ordinals tell us WHERE something is — not how many!"
**App:** Lesson Player — grand-mix word reading activity with a progress tracker showing total words read this month.
**Reward:** A "20+ Reader" gold medal sticker — only awarded today, making it feel very special.

## Day 5 — Month 1 Celebration and Assessment

**Reading (8 min):** Do a relaxed "reading show" — your child reads 10 words you select (pick their most confident words from the month, mixed across families). Then let them "teach" you by picking a card, saying the sounds slowly, and blending it while you pretend not to know. Say, "You're the teacher now!" Finish by reading a short social story together (3–4 sentences) using only words from this month's families. Say, "You learned to read real words this month — that is a huge deal!"
**Math (5 min):** "Ordinal Parade" — line up 10 toys and practice 1st through 10th (introducing 6th–10th verbally even if not drilled). Then write the numerals 11–20 on a strip as fast as possible — a "writing sprint." Celebrate: "You know all the teen numbers and ordinals — you're ready for kindergarten math!"
**App:** Social Story Reader — loads a personalized illustrated story celebrating learning to read and count.
**Reward:** A "Month 1 Graduate" certificate — draw or print a simple certificate and sign it together. Let them display it on the wall.

## Week 4 Checklist
- [ ] Blends -ug words: bug, hug, mug, rug, tug
- [ ] Blends -un words: fun, run, sun, bun
- [ ] Blends -ut words: cut, but, hut, nut
- [ ] Blends -ed words: red, bed, fed, led
- [ ] Blends -eg words: leg, beg, peg

- [ ] Uses ordinal language correctly: 1st, 2nd, 3rd, 4th, 5th

## Parent Notes
This is the culminating week — the goal of 20+ decodable CVC words is absolutely achievable for a child with this foundation. Don't rush the Day 4 grand mix; take the full 8 minutes and celebrate every word.

Short-e is the trickiest vowel for many children because the sound is subtle. If your child confuses /e/ with /i/ (reading *bed* as "bid"), try the anchor: "E is for egg — /e/!" and compare *bed* and *bid* side by side.

For ordinals, everyday life is the best classroom: "You're 1st in line!" "The dog is 2nd!" "What's 3rd on our list?" Use ordinals during snack, getting dressed, and games all week.

By the end of this week, your child has the phonemic awareness and decoding skills of a late-kindergarten reader and the number knowledge of an early first-grader. Month 2 will build on this with longer word families, blends (bl-, cr-, st-), and the Dolch Pre-K sight word list. Celebrate loudly — this is a real achievement.
