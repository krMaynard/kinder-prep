# Month 1, Week 1 — Phoneme Awareness / Subitizing 1–3

## Goals
- Reading: Segment spoken words into individual phonemes by clapping and tapping; isolate beginning, middle, and ending sounds in 3-phoneme words.
- Math: Recognize dot patterns for 1, 2, and 3 instantly without counting; connect dot patterns to numerals 1–3.

## Day 1 — Clap It Out

**Reading (8 min):** Hold up a stuffed animal or toy and say, "We're going to chop words into tiny sound pieces — like a word ninja!" Use simple CVC words: *cat*, *sun*, *dog*. Say each word slowly while clapping each sound: "/k/ — /a/ — /t/, that's three claps!" Have your child copy you. Try at least six words: *cat, sun, dog, big, red, hot*. Say, "How many sounds does *sun* have? Let's clap and count together!"
**Math (5 min):** Flash a card or draw dots — one dot, then two, then three. Hold it up for just one second and say, "How many? Tell me fast!" Start with one dot to build confidence, then move to two. Don't let them count; encourage a quick shout-out. Celebrate every correct answer with a clap.
**App:** Lesson Player — loads today's clapping and dot-flash activity.
**Reward:** A star sticker on a "Word Ninja" chart — one sticker per day this week.

## Day 2 — Tap Your Fingers

**Reading (8 min):** Say, "Today we tap sounds on our fingers — one finger for each sound!" Model with *big*: hold up your hand and tap your thumb "/b/", pointer "/i/", middle "/g/". Then ask your child, "How many sounds in *big*?" Practice with: *dog, hot, red, cut, pin*. When they get it right, say, "Yes! Three sounds — you're a sound superhero!" Let them pick a word from a small picture card pile and tap it out.
**Math (5 min):** Play "Dot Detective." Show a card with 1, 2, or 3 dots arranged in a clear pattern (like dice). Flash it and ask, "How many?" Then flip to a numeral card (1, 2, or 3) and match them together. Say, "One dot matches the number 1 — see how they're friends?" Do 8–10 rounds, mixing all three amounts.
**App:** Phonics Blender — tap the letter cards for each sound heard, then press Blend to hear the whole word.
**Reward:** A fingerprint stamp (ink pad thumbprint) on the chart for each word they tap correctly — aim for five thumbprints.

## Day 3 — First Sound, Last Sound

**Reading (8 min):** Tell your child, "Today we're going to find the secret first sound and the sneaky last sound in words." Say *cat* slowly and ask, "What's the very first sound?" (/k/). Then, "What's the very last sound?" (/t/). Use these words, one at a time: *sun, dog, big, hot, red*. Use a fun voice — whisper the beginning sound, then shout the ending sound. If they struggle, elongate the first phoneme: "Ssssun — what comes first?"
**Math (5 min):** Use small objects (blocks, coins, or crackers) to build sets of 1, 2, and 3. Place them on a mat and ask, "Without counting, how many?" Then ask them to build their own set of 2 (or 1, or 3) to match a dot card you show. This builds quantity sense alongside visual recognition.
**App:** Lesson Player — loads today's first/last sound sorting game.
**Reward:** A small gold star on each finger they tapped correctly during Reading; five stars possible.

## Day 4 — Say It Slow, Say It Fast

**Reading (8 min):** Introduce the blending game: "I'll say a word in s-l-o-w motion — you say it fast!" Use fully segmented phonemes: "/d/ — /o/ — /g/" and your child says "dog!" Start with words they know: *cat, sun, big, hot, red, cut*. After each one say, "Say it slow, then say it fast!" Switch roles — let them try to "trick" you by saying sounds and you blend them. Keep the energy playful and quick-paced.
**Math (5 min):** Play "Three in a Flash." Use a set of dot cards (1–3) shuffled. Flash each card and say, "Tell me fast — no counting!" After each round, ask your child to hold up the matching number of fingers. If they count fingers, gently redirect: "Can you just know? Like magic?" This trains automatic recognition.
**App:** Phonics Blender — hear segmented sounds, then tap Blend to confirm the word.
**Reward:** A "Fast Brain" sticker for every blend they get in under three seconds.

## Day 5 — Word Sound Sorting

**Reading (8 min):** Write three columns on a piece of paper labeled with pictures: a cat (3 sounds), a sun (3 sounds), and a bus (3 sounds). Say each of the following words slowly and have your child tap out the sounds, then decide how many fingers: *dog, hot, red, big, cut, pin*. Confirm together: "Dog — /d/ /o/ /g/ — three sounds!" Celebrate that all CVC words have exactly three sounds. Say, "You're an expert at three-sound words!"
**Math (5 min):** Play "Dot Bingo" with a simple 3-square card (dots: 1, 2, 3 in random order). You call out a number (1, 2, or 3) or flash a dot card, and they cover the matching square with a block. First to cover all three wins a cheer. Play three rounds to reinforce all three subitizing patterns.
**App:** Lesson Player — Friday wrap-up activity with a mini celebration animation.
**Reward:** A "Week 1 Champion" badge sticker — let them place it prominently on the chart.

## Week 1 Checklist
- [ ] Can clap/tap the three sounds in simple words (cat, sun, dog)
- [ ] Can isolate the beginning sound in a spoken CVC word
- [ ] Can isolate the ending sound in a spoken CVC word
- [ ] Can blend three spoken phonemes into a word (say-it-fast game)
- [ ] Recognizes dot patterns for 1, 2, and 3 without counting
- [ ] Can match dot cards to numerals 1, 2, and 3

## Parent Notes
This week is all about the ear, not the eye — your child doesn't need to look at print at all during reading time. The goal is building the "phoneme awareness" muscle that makes decoding possible later. If your child already finds 3-phoneme segmenting easy, try 2-syllable words like *napkin* or *basket* as a bonus stretch.

For subitizing, the key is speed — if they start counting dots, gently cover the card and say "Too slow! How many did you see?" The goal is automatic pattern recognition, like recognizing a friend's face. Dot patterns should be arranged like dice faces for consistency.

Keep sessions exactly 15 minutes. End on a win — if the last item is hard, sneak in an easy one so you finish with success. Encourage with: "Your brain is getting stronger every day!"
