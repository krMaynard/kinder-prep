# Harker School — Kindergarten Admission Standards

> **Note:** This document is based on publicly available information and general knowledge of Harker's admissions process. Always verify current requirements directly with the school at admissions@harker.org or via the Harker website.

## Overview

The Harker School (San Jose, CA) is a highly competitive independent school. Kindergarten applicants typically complete a half-day assessment that evaluates overall school readiness across several domains.

## Assessment Domains

### Pre-Literacy
- Letter recognition (uppercase and lowercase)
- Phonemic awareness (rhyming, beginning sounds, blending)
- Basic phonics and decoding (CVC words)
- Sight word recognition
- Print concepts (left-to-right, top-to-bottom, word boundaries)

### Pre-Numeracy
- Rote counting to 20+
- One-to-one correspondence
- Numeral recognition (0–10)
- Basic addition and subtraction concepts within 10
- Shape and pattern recognition
- Sorting, classifying, and basic measurement comparisons

### Cognitive & Reasoning
- Problem-solving and logical thinking
- Sequencing (what comes next?)
- Memory and attention tasks
- Following multi-step directions

### Fine Motor Skills
- Pencil grip and control
- Drawing basic shapes and figures
- Cutting with scissors
- Writing first name

### Social-Emotional Readiness
- Ability to separate from parent/caregiver
- Attention span (~15–20 minutes on a task)
- Listening and following classroom directions
- Basic cooperation and turn-taking

## Key Differentiators for Gifted Applicants

Harker looks beyond minimum readiness. For a gifted child to stand out:
- **Reading:** Progress toward reading simple books, not just decoding isolated words
- **Math:** Demonstrate flexible thinking (multiple strategies), not just rote answers
- **Communication:** Ability to explain reasoning ("How did you figure that out?")
- **Curiosity:** Enthusiasm for learning and asking questions
